Program Focus

What this program aims to achieve

Mental Health and Psychosocial Support creates safe spaces for people dealing with stress, displacement, poverty, conflict, flooding, and other difficult experiences. It recognizes that empowerment is stronger when emotional wellbeing is protected and when participants also gain practical skills that can support dignity, income, and recovery.

Key outcomes

  • check_circle Provide safe listening spaces and basic psychosocial support for vulnerable groups.
  • check_circle Reduce stigma around emotional distress and mental health conversations.
  • check_circle Train community actors to identify stress signs and refer people responsibly.
  • check_circle Strengthen resilience among women, youth, IDPs, and flood-affected families.
  • check_circle Train participants to recycle waste materials into reusable pads, bags, household items, and other useful products.

How we work

The program uses group sessions, individual support, referral guidance, wellbeing education, trauma-informed facilitation, and hands-on vocational sessions. Participants learn to transform waste materials into reusable pads, bags, fashion accessories, household items, and other products that support confidence and livelihood recovery. It does not replace clinical care, but it helps communities respond with compassion, structure, and practical opportunity.
